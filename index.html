Senior Secondary Physics 1.Which of the following is a vector quantity? A. Speed B. Velocity ✅ C. Distance D. Time Reason: A vector possesses both magnitude and direction. Speed is scalar, but velocity requires direction for its complete specification, making it a vector. 2.The S.I. unit of force is: A. Joule B. Newton ✅ C. Watt D. Pascal Reason: By definition from Newton's Second Law (F=ma), the unit of force is kg⋅m/s², which is designated as 1 Newton (N). 3.A body is said to be in equilibrium when: A. It is at rest only B. The resultant force is maximum C. The resultant force is zero ✅ D. The acceleration is maximum Reason: Equilibrium, whether static or dynamic, signifies a state where the net (resultant) force acting on the body is zero (∑F=0). 4.Which instrument is used to measure electric current? A. Voltmeter B. Ammeter ✅ C. Galvanometer D. Hygrometer Reason: An ammeter is the apparatus used to measure electric current, usually calibrated in Amperes. 5.The density of a substance is defined as: A. Mass per unit volume ✅ B. Volume per unit mass C. Weight per unit volume D. Force per unit area Reason: Density is the ratio of the mass of a substance to the volume it occupies: (ρ=m/V). 6.The slope of a velocity–time graph gives: A. Acceleration ✅ B. Speed C. Distance D. Displacement Reason: The slope represents the rate of change of velocity with respect to time (Δv/Δt), which is the definition of acceleration. 7.Which of the following materials is a good conductor of electricity? A. Plastic B. Copper ✅ C. Rubber D. Wood Reason: Copper is a metal with a high concentration of free electrons, which facilitates the easy flow of electric charge. When a body moves in a circular path at constant speed, it experiences: A. Gravitational force B. Rotational force C. Centripetal force ✅ D. Centrifugal force Reason: The centripetal force is the internal, centre-seeking force required to maintain circular motion by constantly changing the body's direction (velocity). The rate of doing work is called: A. Energy B. Power ✅ C. Momentum D. Efficiency Reason: Power is defined as the work done per unit time: (P=W/t). Which of the following is a renewable source of energy? A. Coal B. Natural Gas C. Solar energy ✅ D. Petroleum Reason: Solar energy is continuously replenished and is effectively inexhaustible, unlike fossil fuels. The device used to convert mechanical energy into electrical energy is: A. Transformer B. Generator ✅ C. Battery D. Motor Reason: A generator operates on the principle of electromagnetic induction to convert rotational (mechanical) motion into electrical energy. The speed of light in air is approximately: A. 3.0×10⁸ m/s ✅ B. 3.0×10⁵ m/s C. 3.3×10² m/s D. 1.5×10⁸ m/s Reason: The speed of light in a vacuum or air is a fundamental constant, approximately 3.0×10⁸ m/s. An image formed by a plane mirror is always: A. Virtual and upright ✅ B. Real and inverted C. Diminished D. Magnified Reason: An image formed by a plane mirror cannot be projected onto a screen (virtual), is the same height, and is upright. The pressure at a point in a liquid increases with: A. Altitude B. Depth ✅ C. Temperature D. Volume Reason: Fluid pressure is given by P=ρgh. Therefore, pressure increases linearly with depth (h). A stone and a feather will fall at the same rate in: A. A vacuum ✅ B. Air C. Water D. Oil Reason: In a vacuum, there is no air resistance, ensuring all objects fall under gravity with the same constant acceleration, regardless of mass. The energy stored in a stretched spring is: A. Kinetic energy B. Potential energy ✅ C. Electrical energy D. Thermal energy Reason: Energy stored due to the change in shape (or position) is potential energy; in this case, elastic potential energy. Which of these quantities has both magnitude and direction? A. Work B. Energy C. Force ✅ D. Pressure Reason: Force requires both a magnitude (how large the push/pull is) and a direction for its complete description, making it a vector quantity. When a substance changes from solid to gas directly, the process is called: A. Condensation B. Evaporation C. Sublimation ✅ D. (Speed, Pressure, and Energy are scalar). A ball is thrown vertically upward and returns to the hand 3.0 s later. Find its initial velocity (g=10 m/s²). A. 15 m/s ✅ B. 30 m/s C. 10 m/s D. 7.5 m/s Reason: The time taken to reach the maximum height is tup=½ttotal=½(3.0s)=1.5s. At maximum height, the final velocity (v) is 0 m/s. Kinetic energy Reason: Since Work done is calculated as Force × Displacement (W=F⋅d), the area under the F−d graph gives the Work done. A car of mass 1000 kg accelerates from rest to 30 m/s in 5.0 s.
